About me

I am a final year Ph.D. Candidate & Teaching Associate in the Department of Econometrics & Business Statistics at Monash University, Australia and a member of the Stochastic Engineering Dynamics Lab at Columbia University, USA.

Before I started my current program, I was a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Mathematical Sciences at the University of Liverpool, UK, and as a member of the EPSRC and ESRC Centre for Doctoral Training (CDT) in Risk & Uncertainty, I held a Postgraduate Research Studentship (2015 - 2019).

I received my first degree (4 year studies) in Mathematics from the University of Athens (2015) and was awarded the M.Res. degree with Distinction (highest achievable grade) from the University of Liverpool, UK (2016).

Research

INTERESTS

Although my research mainly focuses on Probability Theory, my interests in Mathematics are rather broad, ranging from classical Calculus and Measure Theory to Algorithms and Stochastic Calculus. Under the supervision of Athanasios A. Pantelous, Ph.D (Monash University, Australia) and Ioannis A. Kougioumtzoglou, Ph.D (Columbia University, USA), I am currently working on developing efficient approximate techniques for studying Stochastic Differential Equations (SDEs), finding fast approximate solutions especially for non-linear SDEs.

Another current research topic deals with Stochastic Partial Differential Equations (SPDEs), whereas at a later stage I expect to expand my research in other fields, answering questions such as the optimal choice of estimation methods based on available data or delving deeper into concept such as the "Price of Anarchy", among others.
